Datasheet: Patient Outcomes Dataset

Prepared by: LexiTech AI Research · Author: Leslie Amadi

1. Motivation

The Patient Outcomes Dataset was created to support research into the early detection and monitoring of chronic illnesses using anonymised electronic health record (EHR) data. It provides a structured, multi-centre view of patient trajectories, enabling fair, transparent, and traceable machine learning evaluation in healthcare settings.

The dataset is intended for healthcare organisations, research teams, and ML practitioners who need a realistic but privacy-preserving foundation for risk prediction models, treatment outcome analysis, and bias evaluation across demographic groups.

Clinical note: The dataset is designed for model development and evaluation, not for direct point-of-care decision making. Any models trained using this dataset must be clinically validated and governed before deployment.

2. Dataset Summary

2.1 Population & Coverage

The dataset includes longitudinal records from adult patients receiving care at seven hospital centres in a mixed urban–suburban healthcare network.

Attribute Details
Number of patients ~520,000 unique anonymised patient records
Institutions 7 medical centres (acute care & outpatient clinics)
Geography Single country; mix of urban, peri-urban, and rural catchment areas
Time span 8-year window of historical data, aggregated by month
Age range 18–95+ at index encounter

2.2 Record Types

The dataset contains the following broad feature groups:

  • Demographics: age band, sex, broad region, encounter type
  • Clinical measurements: blood pressure, BMI, heart rate, temperature
  • Medical history: diagnosis codes, comorbidities, prior admissions
  • Lab tests: key lab values (e.g. creatinine, HbA1c, eGFR, lipids)
  • Lifestyle indicators: smoking status, high-level adherence flags, activity category

2.3 Target Labels

Labels are defined at the patient-level over a fixed follow-up window and derived from clinician-confirmed diagnoses and outcome events.

Label Type Definition
high_risk Binary (0/1) Patient had at least one serious chronic condition confirmed within 12 months of index date.
readmission_30d Binary (0/1) Readmission to any of the network hospitals within 30 days of discharge.
mortality_1y Binary (0/1) All-cause mortality within 1 year of index encounter (if available in source systems).

3. Schema & Feature Groups

3.1 High-Level Schema

Feature Group Examples Notes
Demographics age_band, sex, region, insurance_type Highly aggregated to reduce re-identification risk.
Vitals & Measurements bmi, sbp, dbp, heart_rate Most recent measurement in a defined lookback window.
Lab Results creatinine, hba1c, egfr, ldl Normalised units; extreme outliers filtered or clipped.
Comorbidities diabetes_flag, ckd_flag, chf_flag Derived from diagnosis codes aggregated into condition flags.
Lifestyle Indicators smoking_status, activity_level, adherence_score Partially self-reported, partially inferred from encounters.
Outcome Labels high_risk, readmission_30d, mortality_1y Clinician-validated where possible; otherwise derived from EHR events.

3.2 Data Splits

Data is split by patient (not by encounter) to avoid leakage across train/test partitions.

Split Percentage Approx. Patients Usage
Train 70% ~364,000 Model development
Validation 15% ~78,000 Hyperparameter tuning & early stopping
Test 15% ~78,000 Final model evaluation and reporting

4. Collection Process & Governance

4.1 Source Systems

  • Hospital electronic health record (EHR) exports (inpatient + outpatient).
  • Laboratory information systems (LIS) for structured lab results.
  • Admission/Discharge/Transfer (ADT) feeds for encounter and outcome events.

4.2 Collection Workflow

  • Raw EHR tables are extracted by each participating centre on a scheduled basis.
  • Data is transferred to a secure research enclave using encrypted channels.
  • Identifiers (names, addresses, direct IDs) are removed at the source institution.
  • Site-level mapping scripts harmonise codes and formats into a shared schema.

4.3 Governance

  • Dataset creation approved by each centre’s internal data governance board.
  • Use is restricted to research or ML evaluation under an approved protocol.
  • Re-identification attempts are explicitly prohibited in usage agreements.
PHI/PII handling: Direct patient identifiers are removed before the data reaches the central environment. Only highly aggregated demographic variables are retained.

5.2 Key Preprocessing Steps

  • Schema standardisation: Derived fields get consistent names across sites (e.g. sbp, bmi).
  • Outlier removal: Clinically impossible values (e.g. negative heart rates) are removed or corrected.
  • Normalisation: Selected numeric features are scaled for ML training (stored as both raw and normalised where needed).
  • Missing value imputation: Combination of simple imputation (median) and model-based methods for selected labs.
  • Category encoding: Categorical variables are one-hot or ordinal encoded, with an explicit unknown bucket.

6. Intended Uses

The dataset is primarily designed for research and development of clinical risk prediction and outcome modelling, such as:

  • Training supervised ML models for early detection of chronic conditions.
  • Developing readmission risk scores and triage prioritisation tools.
  • Benchmarking model performance across demographic subgroups.
  • Evaluating fairness metrics (e.g. equal opportunity, calibration) in healthcare ML systems.

7. Out-of-Scope Uses

The dataset is not intended for:

  • Direct, individual patient diagnosis or real-time treatment decisions.
  • Use as the sole evidence in clinical or legal decision-making.
  • Insurance pricing, eligibility decisions, or risk scoring for coverage.
  • Any application aiming to infer identity or re-identify individuals.

8. Bias, Fairness & Ethical Considerations

8.1 Known Risks

  • Demographic underrepresentation: Some age groups and regions are underrepresented, which may lead to less reliable models for those subpopulations.
  • Healthcare access bias: The dataset reflects patients who had contact with the healthcare system, not the general population.
  • Incomplete social determinants: Important contextual factors (income, housing stability) are not directly encoded.

8.2 Recommended Fairness Checks

  • Evaluate model performance separately across sex, age bands, and regional groups.
  • Monitor calibration curves across demographic subgroups.
  • Document any performance trade-offs when applying fairness constraints.

8.3 Ethical Use Guidance

  • Use the dataset as one input into a broader clinical and ethical evaluation process.
  • Engage clinical stakeholders when interpreting model behaviour and limitations.
  • Clearly communicate known dataset limitations in any downstream model documentation.
Important: Models derived from this dataset should undergo external validation and local calibration before being used in any clinical decision-support context.

9. Distribution & Access Controls

  • Access scope: Internal research and approved collaborations only.
  • Access control: Role-based access via institutional identity provider.
  • Data location: Hosted in a secure research environment with audited access.
  • Retention: Default retention period of 5 years, subject to governance review.

External sharing is only permitted in aggregated or synthetic form and must be reviewed by the data governance board on a case-by-case basis.

10. Maintenance & Versioning

10.1 Update Cadence

  • Major dataset refresh planned annually to incorporate new cohorts and updated lab ranges.
  • Minor quality fixes can be released as patch versions (e.g. 1.0.1, 1.0.2).
  • Deprecated records are flagged rather than removed, to preserve auditability.

10.2 Version History
