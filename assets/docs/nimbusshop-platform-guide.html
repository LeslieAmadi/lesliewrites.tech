The business problem

A shop floor handles real cash and real players all day. A new admin starts on Monday and has to learn, from whoever's free, how to register a device, credit a player's wallet, pay out a withdrawal, lock a machine when someone takes a break, and balance the till at closing. Get any of it wrong and you have a cash discrepancy, an unhappy player, or in a regulated gaming environment a compliance problem.

Why this guide exists

When the operating procedure lives only in experienced staff's heads, every new shop and every new hire is a risk: cash handled inconsistently, transactions logged differently, an audit that can't be reconstructed. This guide makes the entire operating model explicit; who can do what, exactly how money moves, and what gets logged so any shop runs the same safe, auditable way from day one.

How NimbusShop works

NimbusShop runs physical game shops. A Super Admin creates shop locations and assigns administrators; a Shop Admin runs the floor registering the devices players use, managing player wallets, and handling cash in and out. Every device is a access point where a player can see their balance, deposit, play, and lock the machine during a break. The whole system keeps a real-time ledger so balances and reports always match the cash.

EntityWhat it is
ShopA physical venue (location) created by a Super Admin.
DeviceA registered machine in a shop where players play and see their wallet.
Player walletThe real-time balance a player deposits into, plays from, and withdraws from.
LedgerThe transaction record behind every deposit, withdrawal, win, and loss.

Roles & access

The role model is the foundation of safe operation: access must match responsibility, so a Shop Admin can run their floor fully but can't reach across shops or create venues. The documentation makes the boundary explicit as a permission matrix.

CapabilitySuper AdminShop Admin
Create shops / locationsYesNo
Assign administrators to shopsYesNo
Register & activate devicesAll shopsOwn shop
Manage player walletsAll shopsOwn shop
Process deposits / withdrawalsAll shopsOwn shop
View reportsOrg-wideOwn shop
⚠ Access matches accountability

A Shop Admin is accountable for their shop's cash, so they hold full control of their shop and nothing beyond it. This single boundary own-shop scope, no cross-shop or venue-creation rights is what keeps responsibility and authority aligned and makes any discrepancy traceable to one accountable operator.

Device lifecycle

A device moves through a clear lifecycle, and the documentation makes each transition a defined step with an owner.

StateMeaningWho acts
RegisteredAdded to a shop's records, not yet playableShop Admin
ActiveActivated and linked to the shop gameplay, wallet, and reporting enabledShop Admin
LockedTemporarily disabled (e.g. player on a break) balance preservedPlayer / Admin
ReassignedPlayer moved to another device if one is unresponsiveShop Admin
Lock, don't lose

Locking is a small feature with a big trust payoff: a player can step away knowing their balance and session are preserved, and the machine can't be misused while they're gone. Documenting lock/unlock as a first-class, audited action not an afterthought is part of what makes the floor trustworthy.

💰

Player wallet & cash flow

This is the heart of the platform and the part where mistakes cost real money. The documentation walks the full flow cash in, play, cash out with the safeguard that protects the shop at each step.

The wallet flow. Cash is always verified before a wallet is credited; every deposit, withdrawal, win, loss, and lock writes a timestamped ledger entry stamped with the operator so the books always reconcile to the cash drawer.

Verify cash before crediting always

The single most important rule in the whole guide: the admin confirms the physical cash before the wallet is credited, never after. It's the difference between a shop whose ledger always matches the drawer and one that's perpetually chasing a discrepancy. The documentation puts this rule first in every money-handling procedure.

Daily operations & reconciliation

The guide turns the operating model into a routine any admin can run, and ends the day with a reconciliation that catches problems before they compound.

Daily checklist (Shop Admin)
  • Log in with credentials and MFA.
  • Register / activate devices; lock unused ones.
  • Process deposits and withdrawals against verified cash.
  • Check balances on request.
  • Run financial and compliance reports at closing.
End-of-day reconciliation
  • Every device shows the correct status.
  • All wallet transactions are logged and consistent.
  • Report totals for deposits, withdrawals, and GGR match the physical cash log within tolerance (e.g. 0.5%).
  • Any variance is escalated, not absorbed.
🔧

Common errors & quick fixes

The most-needed page in any operational guide. Each entry pairs a real floor problem with the safe response.

ProblemQuick fix
Deposit not recordedRetry; if duplication is possible, stop and escalate to Super Admin for reconciliation rather than risk a double credit.
Device unresponsiveDeactivate it in the dashboard, reassign the player to another device, and log a support ticket.
Balance mismatchExport transaction history, reconcile deposits/withdrawals, and check for pending holds.
Unauthorised unlock attemptBlock the action, confirm player identity, and escalate.
When in doubt about money, stop and escalate

The guide is explicit that a possible duplicate transaction is never “just retried.” The cost of a blocked transaction is a minute of delay; the cost of a double credit is a real loss and a reconciliation headache. Escalation is the safe default whenever cash integrity is in question.

Audit, logging & compliance

In a regulated environment, an action that wasn't logged effectively didn't happen in a way you can defend. The documentation makes the logging contract explicit.

RequirementDetail
Every money / state event is loggedDeposits, withdrawals, and lock/unlock each create a timestamped audit record.
Records identify who and whereEach entry carries the operator ID and the device/shop context.
RetentionEnd-of-day reports and logs are retained per the regulatory retention policy.
Exceptions are approvedAny deviation from procedure must be approved in advance by the document approver.

Key learnings

Access should mirror accountability.

The cleanest part of the role model was the rule that a Shop Admin controls their shop fully and nothing beyond its authority and responsibility in one scope.

Put the money rule first.

“Verify cash before crediting” appears at the top of every wallet procedure, because it's the one rule whose violation costs real money.

Logging is a feature, not a footnote.

Documenting what every action records up front is what turned the platform from operable into auditable.

📝 Writer's note

A product guide for a cash-handling floor isn't really about the software it's about trust and money. I structured this around the operator's day and the points where real cash changes hands, because that's where mistakes are expensive and where clear documentation earns its keep.

The move that mattered most was elevating two rules to first-class status: verify cash before crediting, and log every event with an operator and context. Those two lines are what let a shop reconcile its drawer every night and defend its books in an audit the difference between a platform that works and one a regulated business can actually run on.
