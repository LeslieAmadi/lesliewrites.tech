Role & Outcomes

Overview

This document describes UX Writing Empty State | Leslie Amadi and is written from the perspective of a technical writer supporting engineers, product, and operations.

My Role

Planned the structure, authored the documentation, and aligned the content with stakeholders so it can be used as a repeatable reference and onboarding asset.

Audience

Primarily software engineers, data/ML practitioners, and product or operations teams who need a clear view of behaviour, integration steps, and edge cases.

Scope

In scope: product behaviour, configuration, integration flows, and operational considerations. Out of scope: commercial terms, team processes, and non-technical marketing copy.

Prerequisites

Comfortable reading technical documentation and basic familiarity with the surrounding stack (e.g. APIs, data pipelines, dashboards, or game engines, depending on context).

Impact

Intended to reduce back-and-forth clarification, speed up onboarding, and make future changes safer by giving teams a single, well-structured source of truth.

A gentle, guiding empty state experience for a financial dashboard. Clear tone, calm emotion, and helpful next steps.

Empty State — “No Transactions Yet”

When users first open a financial dashboard, there’s no data to show. Instead of showing a blank page, we use this moment to build trust and help them take the right action.

UX Goal: Reassure the user, reduce confusion, and offer a clear next step without pressure.

Final UX Copy

No activity yet

When you make your first payment or transfer, your activity will appear here.

Make a transfer

Design Principles

  • Human tone: Calm and friendly, not technical.
  • Reassurance: “Activity will appear here” prevents confusion.
  • Single CTA: Avoids overwhelming the user.
