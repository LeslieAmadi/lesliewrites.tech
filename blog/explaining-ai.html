Explaining AI Clearly: A Writer’s Perspective

How I approach making machine learning concepts readable and human-centred

By Leslie Amadi | Published 2025

Why “Clear AI Writing” Matters

Artificial intelligence is powerful — but to most readers, it can feel abstract, technical, or even intimidating. As a writer, my goal isn’t to show how clever AI is; it’s to show how understandable it can be.

The bridge between complex algorithms and human understanding is built with words — structured, intentional, empathetic words. That’s where technical writing becomes more than documentation; it becomes translation.

Step 1: Start with Human Context

Before I write a single sentence about a model, dataset, or algorithm, I start with why it matters to humans. For example, when documenting an AI-based healthcare tool, I don’t begin with technical detail.

“This system helps doctors detect early patterns of disease before symptoms appear.”

That one sentence tells a story — a human-centred one. Once the reader connects emotionally, they’re ready to explore the technical “how.”

Step 2: Layer the Complexity

AI has layers — so should the writing. I follow what I call the “spiral explanation” approach:

  • Begin with the concept in plain language.
  • Add one layer of technical detail.
  • Reconnect it to the real-world application.
“The model learns by identifying patterns in past data — similar to how we learn from experience. Technically, this process uses a neural network trained on thousands of medical images.”

One flows into the other naturally.

Step 3: Define, But Don’t Drown

Definitions are essential — but walls of definitions aren’t. When I introduce a new AI term, I define it once, in everyday language, and then use it confidently throughout the piece.

“A parameter is just a value the model adjusts while learning — like fine-tuning a musical instrument.”

Step 4: Write for Curiosity, Not Just Accuracy

AI writing can be technically correct and still unreadable. So I write with curiosity in mind — inviting the reader to explore rather than endure.

“After a few fine-tuning steps (called batch normalization), the model reached 97% accuracy — meaning it got most predictions right.”

Same fact, softer landing.

Step 5: Respect the Reader’s Learning Journey

My favourite feedback is when a reader says, ‘I finally understood that after reading your explanation.’

When we write about AI, we’re not simplifying intelligence — we’re democratizing it. Because understanding is the first step toward trust.

Closing Thoughts

Explaining AI clearly isn’t just about writing; it’s about empathy. We’re helping people make sense of a fast-changing world, one paragraph at a time.

In a field obsessed with precision, good writing reminds us that clarity is also a form of accuracy — and that every algorithm still needs a human voice to be heard.
