What is Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)?

Published on: 5 November 2025 ‚Ä¢ By Leslie Amadi

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is a security process where users provide two different types of verification to prove their identity. It adds an extra layer of protection to your accounts, ensuring that even if someone knows your password, they still cannot log in without the second factor.

Passwords alone are no longer enough. Cyber attackers often steal or guess credentials through phishing, data leaks, or weak password practices. 2FA reduces this risk by requiring:

  • Something you know: Password or PIN
  • Something you have: Phone, authenticator app, or email code

  • Email or SMS codes
  • Authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy)
  • Hardware security keys (YubiKey)
